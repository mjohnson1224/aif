Welcome to AIFYP Bay Area Chapter

AIF’s mission is to accelerate social and economic change in India. By mobilizing people and resources in the United States, AIF has invested in over 100 Indian non-governmental organizations since its inception in 2001. AIF partners with non-governmental organizations working in the fields of education, livelihood and public health projects in India - with emphases on elementary education, women’s empowerment and maternal and child health respectively. President Bill Clinton serves as Honorary Chair of AIF

Who We Are

We are young, up-and-coming changemakers who believe that awareness building, fundraising, and service can alter the course of India's future.

Since 2009, the Bay Area chapter has empowered young professionals to address social and economic development issues in South Asia and beyond. To date, we've raised over $65,000 for underprivileged children. And we've organized educational and social events that thousands have attended.

Digital Equalizer

We envision an India where all children have access to technology and information to prepare them for the emerging Digital Age.

As we enter the 21st century, technology is infiltrating nearly every facet of our lives. Eight of the ten fastest growing occupations are expected to be computer-related. Despite its unprecedented growth, India faces daunting challenges in the provision of equitable, quality education that prepares its youth for participation in the Digital Age. The Digital Equalizer program supports, empowers, and enables economically disadvantaged children in grades 6-10 to learn how to use the computer as a learning enhancement tool and develop the tehcnical skills that provide a future for their future career in the global market.

What is AIFBAYP?

AIFBAYP is the Bay Area Young Professionals chapter of the national AIF organization. AIF was born out of relief efforts following the Gujarat earthquake of 2001, and found an outpouring of support from Americans concerned about India's development. Following the initial effort, AIF built on donor interest and the needs in the community to build a nationwide platform.
